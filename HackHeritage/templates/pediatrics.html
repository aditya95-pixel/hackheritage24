Pediatrics

Overview

Pediatrics is a branch of medicine that involves the medical care of infants, children, and adolescents. The age limit typically ranges from birth up to 18 years. Pediatricians are dedicated to ensuring the health and well-being of young patients, providing preventive care, diagnosis, and treatment of illnesses.

Neonatology

Neonatology is a subspecialty of pediatrics that focuses on the medical care of newborns, particularly those who are ill or premature. It involves managing conditions such as:

  • Respiratory distress syndrome
    Respiratory distress syndrome is a condition in newborns where the lungs are not fully developed, causing difficulty in breathing.
  • Premature birth
    Premature birth refers to babies born before 37 weeks of gestation, which may lead to various health issues due to underdeveloped organs.
  • Congenital disorders
    Congenital disorders are conditions present at birth, including heart defects, cleft lip/palate, and genetic abnormalities.

Pediatric Oncology

Pediatric oncology deals with the diagnosis and treatment of cancers in children. This includes conditions like:

  • Leukemia
    Leukemia is a type of cancer that affects the blood and bone marrow, causing abnormal blood cell production.
  • Brain tumors
    Brain tumors in children can affect brain function and may require surgery, radiation, or chemotherapy.
  • Neuroblastoma
    Neuroblastoma is a cancer that develops from immature nerve cells and is most common in children under 5 years old.

Pediatric Cardiology

Pediatric cardiology involves diagnosing and treating heart conditions in children, such as:

  • Congenital heart defects
    Congenital heart defects are structural problems with the heart present at birth, such as holes between the heart chambers.
  • Arrhythmias
    Arrhythmias are irregular heartbeats that can lead to symptoms like dizziness or fainting and may need treatment to correct.
  • Heart murmurs
    Heart murmurs are unusual sounds heard during a heartbeat, which can be benign or indicate underlying heart conditions.

Pediatric Neurology

Pediatric neurology focuses on treating neurological disorders in children, including:

  • Epilepsy
    Epilepsy is a neurological disorder characterized by recurrent seizures, which may require medication for control.
  • Cerebral palsy
    Cerebral palsy is a group of disorders affecting movement and muscle tone due to brain damage occurring before or at birth.
  • Developmental delays
    Developmental delays refer to when a child does not reach developmental milestones at the expected age, affecting cognitive, motor, or social skills.
