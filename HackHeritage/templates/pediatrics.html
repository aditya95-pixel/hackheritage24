Pediatrics

Overview

Pediatrics is a branch of medicine that involves the medical care of infants, children, and adolescents.

Neonatology

Neonatology focuses on medical care of newborns, particularly those who are ill or premature.

  • Respiratory distress syndrome
    Difficulty in breathing due to underdeveloped lungs in newborns.
  • Premature birth
    Babies born before 37 weeks, often facing various health challenges.
  • Congenital disorders
    Conditions present at birth like heart defects or genetic abnormalities.

Pediatric Oncology

Pediatric oncology focuses on diagnosing and treating cancers in children.

  • Leukemia
    A cancer affecting the blood and bone marrow.
  • Brain tumors
    Tumors affecting brain function, requiring specialized treatments.
  • Neuroblastoma
    A cancer developing from immature nerve cells, common in young children.

Pediatric Cardiology

Pediatric cardiology focuses on diagnosing and treating heart conditions in children.

  • Congenital heart defects
    Heart structure issues present from birth.
  • Arrhythmias
    Irregular heartbeats causing dizziness or fainting.
  • Heart murmurs
    Unusual heart sounds that may indicate underlying issues.

Pediatric Neurology

Pediatric neurology deals with treating neurological disorders in children.

  • Epilepsy
    A disorder causing recurrent seizures, managed through medication.
  • Cerebral palsy
    A group of disorders affecting movement and muscle tone.
  • Developmental delays
    Delays in reaching cognitive, motor, or social milestones.
