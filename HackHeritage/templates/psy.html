Career Path in Psychology

Explore Various Fields in Psychology

  • Clinical Psychology
    Clinical psychologists diagnose and treat mental, emotional, and behavioral disorders. They use psychological techniques to help patients manage conditions like anxiety, depression, and more.
  • Cognitive Psychology
    Cognitive psychologists study how people understand, think, and remember information. They focus on mental processes such as perception, memory, reasoning, and problem-solving.
  • Developmental Psychology
    Developmental psychologists study the psychological development of individuals throughout their lives. This can include children, adolescents, adults, and the elderly.
  • Forensic Psychology
    Forensic psychologists apply psychological principles to legal matters, often working in criminal justice or law enforcement settings.
  • Health Psychology
    Health psychologists study the psychological and behavioral processes in health, illness, and healthcare. They work to promote healthy living and improve patient outcomes.
  • Industrial-Organizational Psychology
    Industrial-organizational psychologists apply psychological principles to workplace environments, focusing on improving productivity, employee well-being, and organizational success.
  • Neuropsychology
    Neuropsychologists study the relationship between the brain and behavior, often working with patients who have experienced brain injuries or neurological disorders.
