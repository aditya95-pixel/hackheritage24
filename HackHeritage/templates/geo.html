Geography Career Path Guidance

About Geography

Geography is the study of places and the relationships between people and their environments. It covers physical properties of the Earth's surface and the human societies spread across it, and it also examines how human culture interacts with the natural environment.

Career Opportunities

  • Urban Planner

    Urban Planner

    Urban planners develop plans and programs for land use in urban areas, focusing on the development and growth of cities and communities.

  • Cartographer

    Cartographer

    Cartographers create maps and geographic data to assist in navigation, planning, and understanding spatial relationships.

  • Geospatial Analyst

    Geospatial Analyst

    Geospatial analysts use geographic information systems (GIS) to analyze spatial data and solve problems related to geography and location.

  • Environmental Consultant

    Environmental Consultant

    Environmental consultants assess and provide advice on environmental issues, including pollution, resource management, and regulatory compliance.

  • Geographer

    Geographer

    Geographers study the Earth's landscapes, environments, and the relationships between people and their environments to understand spatial patterns and processes.
