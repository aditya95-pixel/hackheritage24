Physics Career Path Guidance

Career Opportunities in Physics

Explore key areas in physics and associated career opportunities:

Research and Academia

Involves conducting experiments and research to expand knowledge in various branches of physics.

  • Research Scientist
    Conducting cutting-edge experiments to discover new principles of physics.
  • Professor
    Teaching physics at universities and conducting academic research.

Astrophysics

Studying the physics of the universe, including stars, planets, and galaxies.

  • Astrophysicist
    Researching celestial bodies and phenomena using physics principles.
  • Cosmologist
    Focusing on the study of the universe's origin, structure, and evolution.

Medical Physics

Applying physics principles to the medical field to improve diagnostics and treatments.

  • Medical Physicist
    Developing and maintaining medical technologies, such as imaging and radiation therapy.
  • Radiologist
    Using medical imaging technologies like X-rays and MRIs for diagnosis.

Engineering Physics

Combining physics principles with engineering applications to solve real-world problems.

  • Optical Engineer
    Designing and developing optical systems such as lenses, lasers, and fiber optics.
  • Nuclear Engineer
    Working on the design and maintenance of nuclear reactors and other nuclear technologies.
