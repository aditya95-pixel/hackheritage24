Surgery

Overview

The Surgery department is a critical field in medicine that focuses on the diagnosis, treatment, and rehabilitation of patients through surgical procedures. Surgeons work across various specialties to perform operations that can save lives, improve health, and enhance the quality of life.

General Surgery

General Surgery involves a broad range of surgical procedures, often focusing on the abdominal organs, including the stomach, liver, pancreas, and intestines.

  • Appendectomy A surgical procedure to remove the appendix when it is infected or inflamed.
  • Cholecystectomy Surgical removal of the gallbladder, often due to gallstones causing pain or infection.
  • Hernia Repair Surgical correction of a hernia, where an organ or tissue protrudes through a weak spot in the muscle.
  • Gastrointestinal Surgery Surgery to treat conditions affecting the gastrointestinal tract, including the stomach and intestines.

Cardiothoracic Surgery

Cardiothoracic Surgery focuses on surgical treatment of diseases affecting organs inside the thorax (the chest), primarily the heart, lungs, and esophagus.

  • Coronary Artery Bypass Surgery A procedure that improves blood flow to the heart by bypassing blocked or narrowed coronary arteries.
  • Heart Valve Repair/Replacement Surgery to repair or replace a damaged heart valve to restore normal blood flow through the heart.
  • Lung Cancer Surgery Removal of cancerous tissue from the lungs, often as part of a treatment plan for lung cancer.
  • Thoracic Aortic Aneurysm Repair Surgery to repair a weakened area in the wall of the thoracic aorta to prevent rupture.

Neurosurgery

Neurosurgery is the medical specialty concerned with the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of disorders that affect the nervous system, including the brain, spinal cord, and peripheral nerves.

  • Brain Tumor Surgery Surgical removal of tumors from the brain, which can be benign or malignant.
  • Spinal Fusion A surgical procedure to join two or more vertebrae in the spine to eliminate motion and reduce pain.
  • Craniotomy A surgical operation where a bone flap is temporarily removed from the skull to access the brain.
  • Peripheral Nerve Surgery Surgery to treat conditions affecting the peripheral nerves, such as carpal tunnel syndrome.

Orthopedic Surgery

Orthopedic Surgery deals with conditions involving the musculoskeletal system, including bones, joints, ligaments, tendons, and muscles.

  • Joint Replacement Surgical replacement of a damaged or diseased joint with an artificial one.
  • Arthroscopy A minimally invasive surgical procedure used to diagnose and treat joint problems.
  • Spinal Surgery Surgical procedures to treat spinal disorders, including herniated discs and scoliosis.
  • Fracture Repair Surgical intervention to fix broken bones and restore their normal alignment and function.
