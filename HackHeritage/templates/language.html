The World of Languages

Exploring the Diversity of Human Communication

Major Language Families

Indo-European Languages

The Indo-European family is one of the largest and most widespread language families. It includes languages like English, Spanish, Hindi, and Russian.

Sino-Tibetan Languages

Sino-Tibetan languages are spoken by over a billion people, mainly in East and Southeast Asia. Notable languages in this family include Mandarin Chinese, Burmese, and Tibetan.

Afro-Asiatic Languages

The Afro-Asiatic language family is native to parts of the Middle East, North Africa, and the Horn of Africa. It includes languages such as Arabic, Hebrew, and Amharic.

Niger-Congo Languages

The Niger-Congo family is one of the largest language families in Africa, with languages like Swahili, Yoruba, and Zulu spoken by millions.

Austronesian Languages

The Austronesian languages are spoken in parts of Southeast Asia, the Pacific Islands, and Madagascar. Examples include Tagalog, Maori, and Hawaiian.
